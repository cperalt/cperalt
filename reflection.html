Professional Development

The professional development component of the gateway served as a refresher of the importance of professionalism in the workplace.

As a realization, I identified one of my strengths to be effective communication. Throughout the gateway, I was effective in relaying information pertaining to questions and concerns regarding the canvas course through email. I used a confident and natural tone while maintaining proper email etiquette.

Technical Section

The technical aspects of programming for me primarily come down to two things: being resourceful enough to find the solutions and a trial-and-error mindset. Majority of the problems I ran into were found with a quick internet search or finding a similar code snippet online. I have come to realize the importance of adaptability and flexibility. I was able to progress and advance my learning by utilizing code examples found online and making them work in my favor. Much of this method involved trial-and-error, playing around with code and testing what worked and did not work. Here I emphasize Technical Program Manager Justin McNeil’s words, “Practice makes progress.”

Personal Section

On a personal level, I have realized the importance of having mentors and being able to rely on others. I really enjoyed attending office hours and having actual interactions with fellow applicants and instructors.

I am very proud about my current level of understanding and progress. I feel like I have advanced so much within a short time frame. Although the learning content on the gateway canvas course has felt slow due to its introductory nature, I took the liberty to advance my learning through external resources, being the source of my learning. What I have realized as I started to code and has helped my advancement, is that I am genuinely interested, enjoying, and having lots of fun.
